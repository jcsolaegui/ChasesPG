Troubleshooting procedure

Below are the steps the Service Desk will follow to diagnose an incident related to this service component. Please consider their perspective when completing this document. Instructions for functional escalation to tier 2/3 support are included at the end or, if the escalation instructions differ depending on the diagnosis, specific escalation information is included at the appropriate point in the diagnosis path. Please feel free to tailor the format to suit the information related to this service.

 

IS&T provides SharePoint services on two platforms:

· SharePoint 2007 on premise

o Production farm URLs begin with https://share.bu.edu/sites/...

o Dev farm URLs begin with https://sp-dev.bu.edu/sites/...

· SharePoint Online (SharePoint 2013) on Microsoft's cloud-based Office 365 platform

o Production shared site URLs begin with https://bushare.sharepoint.com/sites/...

o Production personal site URLs begin with https://bushare-my.sharepoint.com/personal/...

 

Other departments run their own SharePoint platforms, e.g., the Office of Technology Development (OTD) runs a SharePoint server at https://otds.bu.edu/. OTD provides their own client support, but occasionally a client support ticket comes into our Service Desk by mistake – these should be directed back to John Turi (jturi@bu.edu, 617-358-5841) in OTD. As far as I know, OTD has not been onboarded to ServiceNow, so you'll have to use ServiceNow's "Send External Mail" feature. Systems Administration does occasionally help them with server issues – those tickets can be directed to the Systems Administration group.

 

1. Incident, SharePoint 2007 – Access denied and the client has been told that s/he has been granted access to a particular URL in SharePoint 2007

a. Review with the client all the steps in the public-facing troubleshooting page at www.bu.edu/tech/services/comm/collaboration/sharepoint/tips/. Most access denial issues are caused by one of the following:

 i. The client not using the "AD\" prefix when entering a login name, or using a forward slash rather than a backslash, or putting a space after the backslash; or

 ii. The client not entering the full URL given them, e.g., trying to start at the top level (share.bu.edu) or some other level higher than the one to which they have access.

2. Incident, Office 365/SharePoint Online – Access denied and the client has been told that s/he has been granted access to a particular URL in SharePoint Online

a. Review with the client all the steps in the public-facing troubleshooting page at www.bu.edu/tech/services/comm/collaboration/sharepoint/login2013/.

b. If the client has questions about "OneDrive @ Boston University" or Office Online, review with the client the information in the public-facing page at www.bu.edu/tech/services/comm/collaboration/sharepoint/onedrive/.

3. Service Request, SharePoint Online – New site collection

a. Assign to Collaboration Services (until this queue is available - expected mid-January 2016 - continue to assign to Service Management Office)

4. Service Request, SharePoint Online – New site (e.g., a project or group site) within IS&T's existing site collection

a. Assign to Collaboration Services (until this queue is available - expected mid-January 2016 - continue to assign to Service Management Office)

5. Service Request, SharePoint 2007 – Request to be granted access to a resource at a particular URL, and the URL begins with

a. https://share.bu.edu/sites/buw, assign to BUworks Training

b. https://share.bu.edu/sites/ist/appsdev/CM, assign to BUworks Training

c. https://share.bu.edu/sites/ist/appsdev (except for appsdev/CM), assign to Administrative Systems

d. https://share.bu.edu/sites/ist (other than ist/appsdev), assign to Service Management Office

e. https://share.bu.edu/sites/[AnythingElse], it should go to that site collection's administrator, usually someone in the department. If that administrator cannot be identified, assign it to Service Management Office

6. Service Request, SharePoint 2007 – Request to restore from backup

a. Assign to Storage Administration

7. Incident or Service Request from a site collection administrator, SharePoint 2007 or SharePoint Online

a. Assign to Service Management Office

8. Service Request, SharePoint Online – Request to license a person for SharePoint Online or Office Online, or to get a OneDrive in Office 365

a. Assign to Service Management Office (the Service Desk should not assign SharePoint Online or Office Online (formerly Office WebApp) licenses in office 365)

Required information and instructions for escalation

If the incident requires escalation to tier 2/3 support, please make sure the following information is documented in the incident record before escalating. This will facilitate tier 2/3 understanding of the issue and expedite resolution.

1. If the issue is a denial of access to a URL for which the client believes s/he has been granted access:

a. The exact login name entered, including the prefix

b. The exact URL

c. The full text of the error message

d. The platform (e.g., Windows or Mac)

e. The browser

Tier 2/3 escalation groups for this service:

· Tier 2 – See specific instructions above

· Tier 3 – Systems Engineering (Escalate P1s to this group)

 

NOTE – when escalating a P1/Major Incident, please follow the Major Incident escalation process instead.
